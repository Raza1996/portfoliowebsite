DERMAPAY

Type:

Group Project

Role:

Concept Art, Ideation, Illustration

Tools:

Adobe Illustrator, figma

Duration:

1 week

An animation made to advertise a speculative design product ‘DermaPay’. The animation showcases a fictional advertisement made by the city of Vancouver. For this assignment, I was responsible for the creative direction, storyboard, and creation of animation assets.

STORYBOARD

The process began with storyboarding the animation. Storyboards guide the animators through the various scenes that they animate. I storyboarded the animation using a standard storyboarding template consisting of 6 shots per page. Any effective storyboard must use a variety of shots to tell a story. I made sure the movements were easy for the animator to interpret easily.

IDEATION

The next step was sketching out the various designs for the characters. Here is an example of the ideation involved in crafting the character design. The visuals were inspired by ads made by the City of Vancouver youtube channel. I chose this style as they were relatively straightforward to make, simple enough to remember, and economical given how much time we had to make them.

Finally, the individual assets were crafted on Adobe Illustrator. The scenes were straightforward to make as they required combining simple geometric shapes to create an idea of the location where they take place. Backgrounds consisted of solid colors so that all attention would be squarely on the characters.

Challenges

One challenge in creating these assets was figuring out how to have the characters move body parts without having to draw them frame by frame. To remedy this, character assets were created disjointed so that the animator had more freedom in moving them. Multiple versions of the same assets were created to provide the animator with a more diverse array of actions and tasks to work with.

REFLECTION

For this project, I had to deliberately mimic the style of another aesthetic ('City of Vancouver'). It asked me to break down elements that made the original designs work and adapt them to this project. I worked on this project with a strict deadline and also in the middle of shifting homes so I learned some rather valuable lessons in time management as a bonus.
