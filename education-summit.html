MEDIA ARTS

EDUCATION SUMMIT MOTION GRAPHIC

Type:

Individual Project

Role:

Concept Art, Ideation, Illustration, Video Editing

Tools:

Adobe Illustrator, figma, Adobe After Effects

Duration:

1 week

Alhambra US Chambers has hosted a summit for higher education and learning. In this video, I set out to create an infographic that advertised this event by showcasing the diverse range of voices that would be platformed and how the idea of higher education being a higher persuit for all to attain. The end result was received well by staff.

IDEATION

No storyboarding was needed for this project as the message was relatively straightforward. I began by plotting ideas for various prospective students from different Middle Eastern countries. I had to do much research here to ensure that cultural sensitivity was prioritized while portraying a contemporary forward-looking style.

It was decided that the text would be synchronized with royalty-free music.

PRODUCTION

Next was a matter of actually creating all the assets for the video. Vectors of the drawings were created via Adobe Illustrator and later ported to Adobe After Effects for future use.

The color scheme was inspired by Alhambra US chamber's branding color pallete. It worked well with creating the fashion direction of the characters.

The arts style chosen for this project was inspired by Mads Berg's work that employed geometrical elements and gradients to create simple yet iconic characters that are easy to remember and animate for.

EDITING

This part took multiple iterations to complete. Staff at Alhambra came up with several minor and major changes to the script with each new version. I was fortunate enough to be able to accommodate for them.

Even though storyboarding was not needed for this project as I had a clear vision of how to accomplish the motion graphic, on further reflection I conclude that it would have benefited the staff if I did create a short script for them. Regardless, I was proud of my punctuality with each iteration and was excited to have the motion graphic advertised for all to see.
